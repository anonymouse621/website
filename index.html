So, you decided you wanted to sail the high seas? Great! Here's a guide with everything you need to know.

Browser

First, you'll need a browser. I recommend librewolf. It's an open source privacy focused browser based off firefox, and is perfect for our task.

After you've installed librewolf, click on the uBlock origin extension icon (next to the browser menu) and open the dashboard. (If uBlock Origin isn't installed on your browser, search 'uBlock origin' on your preferred extension store.)

While in the dashboard, head over to the filters list tab and tick these boxes. If you scroll a bit further down, you'll find the 'Regions, languages' section. Select 'ru ua uz kz: RU AdList' and your own language(s) there.

Now you'll need to change your DNS settings. Open your browsers settings and open the privacy and security tab. Scroll down until you see 'Enable DNS over HTTPS using:' and select 'Max protection' there. In the 'Choose provider' menu select 'Quad9'.

If you don't use librewolf, set your search engine to https://duckduckgo.com/. It's private and doesn't track your searches.

Your browser is all set up. Now you'll need to choose if you want to use direct download links, or torrents for downloading. Each section has their pros and cons listed.

File archiver

Direct download links

Pros

  • Doesn't require additional software
  • Easier
  • Ability to stream

Cons

  • Very slow download speeds
  • Unreliable
  • Can be taken down by copyright holders
  • Way less content
  • Sketchy sites

For direct download links, you don't need anything else! Just your browser that you've set up earlier. Head over to the resources section and start downloading!

Torrents

Pros

  • Usually very fast with popular files
  • Can't be taken down
  • Ability to download large files
  • Ability to pause and resume downloads

Cons

  • Requires a VPN and torrent client
  • Can expose your IP address when not using a VPN
  • May contain malware if an untrusted source is used

VPN

For torrents, you'll need a VPN first. Without one, your ISP (Internet provider) could get mad at you or even fine you in some countries. Not all VPN's allow torrenting, so be sure to check if yours allows it before torrenting.

I recommend Windscribe or Mullvad. These are relatively cheap, private and allow torrenting. Windscribe has a free plan that is limited to 10GB per month, and is the only (safe) free vpn that allows torrenting.

After you installed a VPN, you'll need to configure a killswitch. In Windscribe this feature is called 'firewall mode'. Open the Windscribe menu and click on the 'connection' button. From here, set 'Firewall mode' to auto and 'when?' to 'before connection'.

Your VPN is all set up! Be sure to enable the killswitch before turning on your VPN and select the closest / best location for fast speeds.

Torrent client

A torrent client is the program you'll use to download files from torrents. qBittorrent is a safe, open-source and easy to use torrent client, go ahead and install it. In the qBittorrent installer, enable these options.

When qBittorrent is done installing, open and enable your VPN. Once you've enabled your VPN, open qBittorrent and open the options menu. In the options menu, click on the advanced button. Here you need to set 'Network interface' to the name of your VPN. Now you're only able to download files when using your VPN, which prevents leaking of your IP adress.

Your torrent client is all set up! Please keep your VPN enabled from now on.

Navigating rutracker

From now on, you'll be able to translate rutracker. Click your browsers menu button on the top right and select 'Translate page...'. set the 'translate from' field to Russian and the 'Translate to' field to your preferred language. Now rutracker should be displayed in your preferred language. (Note: these translations are not that accurate)

To use rutracker, simply type the name of the file/media/etc. you're looking for in the search bar and press enter. Now you'll see a page with results. Here's an image explaining every column. Sorting from highest upvotes to lowest is recommended to find high-quality listings. Do this by clicking the triangle next to the upvotes / S column.
